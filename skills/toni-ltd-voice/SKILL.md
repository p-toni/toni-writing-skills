# toni.ltd Voice

Use this skill to make writing feel like it belongs in `toni.ltd`: private field notes made public, written by a bounded learner building instruments for thought. The voice is not academic, not marketing, not diary, and not generic "clear writing." It is technical intimacy: concepts are load-bearing because they change the author's loop.

## Core Voice

Start from pressure, not topic. The first movement should answer: what became unstable, expensive, tempting, or suspicious in the author's own loop?

Prefer placed paragraphs over smooth exposition. A sentence can stand alone when it is doing structural work.

Use concepts as operators, not ornaments. Good recurring operators here include: map, cut, edge, loop, drift, contact, re-entry, source closed, rebuild, weaken, rotate, harden, feedback, borrowed coherence.

Trust the reader. Do not over-explain why a thought matters if the next paragraph can make the pressure visible.

Theory may enter, but only as a tool that sharpens the personal problem. A citation should behave like an anchor in the constellation, not like permission to sound academic.

## Non-Negotiables

- Write from inside the author's constraints: time, attention, feedback control, memory, mood, compute, context switching.
- Keep short paragraphs when the idea is structural. Fragments are allowed.
- Name the operator. Do not hide it inside a long explanatory clause.
- Preserve first-person stakes. The writing should know why this matters to the author.
- Make every essay leave behind a diagnostic, protocol, or test.
- Prefer "what changes what I do next" over "what sounds complete."
- Let contradiction stay visible. The site likes corrections, revisions, and second thoughts.
- Use backlinks as local memory. They should feel like part of the thought, not SEO.
- Keep claims weaker than the prose wants until contact, feedback, or evidence earns strength.
- Keep the voice consistent and the vessel variable. Do not let every essay become the same correction arc.
- End with a pressure still alive when that is truer than closure.

## Anti-Filler Checklist

Delete or rewrite these patterns:

- The literature-tour paragraph: "X says, Y says, Z says" before the author has a problem.
- The abstract bridge: "This matters because..." followed by generic importance.
- The polished recap: a closing that repeats the essay instead of moving the pressure.
- The thesis announcement: "The key insight is..." before the insight has become necessary.
- The double explanation: two adjacent paragraphs saying the same thing, one conceptual and one plain.
- The detached expert voice: sentences that sound right but could be written by anyone.
- The adjective stack: powerful, nuanced, rich, complex, sophisticated, unless the word is doing exact work.
- The research alibi: citations used to increase authority instead of changing the map.
- The universal claim that should be private: "humans are..." when "I keep..." is more honest.
- The smooth AI paragraph: balanced, complete, correct, and dead.

## House Moves

Use these moves when shaping an essay.

### Same Voice, Different Vessel

Before writing or rewriting, choose the piece's vessel. The house voice stays stable, but the motion should not.

Valid vessels:

- **Correction:** an older map was useful, then a new failure appeared.
- **Control spec:** rules, gates, levels, failure modes, hard stops.
- **Field note:** one lived scene or object first, concept second.
- **Protocol:** mostly tests, checklist, procedure, or operating discipline.
- **Plate essay:** visual-first; images, figures, or spatial forms carry the argument.
- **Fragment sequence:** numbered shards around an unresolved pressure.
- **Dialogue:** claim and counter-claim, or self-cross-exam.
- **Case study:** project/interface/event first, principle second.
- **Glossary:** operators defined as usable tools, each with a wound or consequence.

If two recent essays used the same vessel, choose a different one unless the repetition is intentional.

### Pressure First

Good:

> I keep getting tempted by clean definitions.

Not:

> This essay explores the relationship between information, compression, and bounded cognition.

### Correction Arc

The site often advances by revising an earlier local truth:

1. Older piece gave me a useful map.
2. The map started failing or hardening.
3. This piece names the new failure.
4. A stricter operator replaces the old one.

### Operator Ladder

Compress a chain until it becomes usable:

> point -> edge -> curvature -> test

or:

> prompt -> edit -> guard -> check -> ship -> learn

Use these only when the chain becomes an instrument. Do not decorate.

### Negative Definition

The voice often clarifies by removing false versions:

> Not authority.
> Not identity.
> Not taste.
>
> Load-bearing participation.

This works when the final line lands as a named object.

### Private Diagnostic

Convert claims into tests:

> If I cannot rebuild the structure with the source closed, I do not own it.

or:

> If weakening one sentence destroys the structure, I do not have geometry.

## Audience Registers

### Essay Mode

Use for `content/writing/*.md`.

The prose can be strange, recursive, and intimate. It should feel like the author is installing a stabilizer in himself. Keep citations sparse and integrated. Let headings be simple: `Preamble`, `Thesis`, then numbered sections if useful.

### Work Node Mode

Use for `content/work/*.md`.

Be concrete and proof-oriented. State what the project is, what it makes possible, and where the live proof lives. Keep the same voice, but reduce abstraction.

### Visual Play Node Mode

Use for shaders, sharp, voxel, and visual-only nodes.

Do not force essay chrome. Use a short sentence or visual treatment. These nodes are atmosphere, evidence, or play, not full arguments.

### System Copy

Use compact labels. Prefer nouns and operators over instructions. Avoid explaining the UI inside the UI.

## Calibration Examples

### Example 1: Bounded Me

Strong source line:

> I keep getting tempted by clean definitions.

Lesson: begin with a recurring temptation or failure, not a concept summary. The abstraction arrives after the private pressure.

### Example 2: Geometry Over Retrieval

Strong source line:

> Understanding is what remains when the source is closed.

Lesson: the house voice likes portable tests. A sentence is good when it becomes a tool the reader can run.

### Example 3: Weak Geometry

Strong source lines:

> Geometry is not enough.
>
> I need weak geometry.

Lesson: let the correction breathe. Do not pad the turn with transition prose.

### Example 4: Allowed Ignorance

Strong source line:

> Failure often looks less like chaos than like the return of a difference I stopped paying for.

Lesson: abstract concepts need a felt consequence. "Difference" becomes real because it returns as failure.

### Example 5: The World Answers

Current mismatch:

> Peirce's pragmatic test helps here...
> Cybernetics begins where...
> Ashby's law...
> Noe's account...

Lesson: the content is right, but too many sections open from tradition. Rewrite so the author's contact problem opens the door and the source enters after the pressure is already alive.

Better direction:

> I built maps because retrieval was too cheap.
>
> Then I found the next failure.
>
> A map can still lie.
>
> Not by being wrong.
> By never being touched.

## Editing Workflow

1. Identify the private pressure. If it is missing, write that before polishing anything.
2. Choose the vessel. Name it before drafting: correction, control spec, field note, protocol, plate essay, fragment, dialogue, case study, or glossary.
3. Mark imported theory. For each citation-heavy paragraph, ask: what personal operator does this source sharpen?
4. Cut the literature-tour order. Move sources after the author's claim when possible.
5. Convert explanations into diagnostics. Ask: what can future-me test, weaken, rebuild, or notice?
6. Shorten the structural lines. Let them stand alone.
7. Preserve useful strangeness. Do not normalize the prose into professional essay voice.
8. Check the ending. It should either install a rule, expose a live pressure, or name the next operator.

## Before Returning A Revision

Check:

- Does the piece sound like it came from this site, not from a smart research memo?
- Does every cited tradition change the author's map?
- Are there at least three lines that could become node labels, diagnostics, or constellation anchors?
- Did this piece choose a distinct vessel, or is it repeating the last essay's motion by habit?
- Did the edit preserve uncertainty where the map has not earned closure?
- Did the edit reduce smoothness when smoothness was hiding borrowed confidence?
